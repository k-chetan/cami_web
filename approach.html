Who is Therapy For?

Therapy generally helps to increase self-knowledge and self-understanding, to manage interpersonal difficulties such as romantic, family or professional relationships, to relieve tension, to promote change, and to improve mental health as a whole.

The increase in scientific research in the area and the development of evidence-based therapies have helped to spread the importance of therapy for mental health.

Psychotherapy is a safe space to dive into the origin of certain patterns of emotions and behavior, and also to process challenges in the present moment.

My Methods

I use internationally recognized therapy models such as IFS and EFT. I also have training in Somatic Experiencing (SE), focusing on the body's experience and the wisdom of the nervous system.

Internal Family Systems (IFS)

We all have internal "parts" — aspects of our personality that carry different emotions, beliefs, and roles. Some parts may protect us, while others hold the pain of past experiences.

IFS is a compassionate approach that helps you understand and heal these different parts, bringing curiosity and self-compassion to your inner world.

Somatic Experiencing (SE)

Developed by Dr. Peter Levine, SE treats trauma by focusing on the body's physiological responses rather than just memory.

It focuses on observing bodily sensations, helping the nervous system gradually regain balance and release accumulated survival energy.

Emotion-Focused Therapy (EFT)

EFT views emotions as gateways to our subjective experience. Sadness, anxiety, or anger are messengers that some emotional need is not being met.

Research shows EFT is highly effective for strengthening relationships and resolving trauma by accessing and transforming emotions.

Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy

Combines cognitive therapy with mindfulness to help recognize automatic thought patterns, especially those related to anxiety and depression.

I incorporate exercises from the 8-week MBCT protocol into sessions to support the access and expression of internal experience.
