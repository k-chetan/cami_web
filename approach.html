Who is Therapy For?

Therapy generally helps to increase self-knowledge, to manage interpersonal difficulties such as romantic, family or professional relationships, to relieve tension, and to promote change.

Psychotherapy is a safe space to dive into the origin of certain patterns of emotions and behavior, and also to process challenges in the present moment.

My Methods

I use internationally recognized therapy models such as DBR and IFS. Both are non-pathologizing approaches incorporating memory reconsolidation.

I continuously update myself through readings from key figures like Bessel van der Kolk & Gabor Maté.

Deep Brain Reorienting (DBR)

Focuses on the brainstem's orienting response to trauma. It processes the initial physiological shock before emotion or meaning attaches.

This modality is highly effective for deep-seated attachment trauma and persistent triggers.

Internal Family Systems (IFS)

We all have internal "parts". Some protect us, others hold pain. IFS is a compassionate and empowering approach that helps you understand and heal these different parts.

By connecting with your core "Self" — the calm, wise, and centered part of you — you can bring healing and harmony to your internal system.

Somatic Experiencing (SE)

Based on the understanding that trauma is not just stored in memory, but also in the body's physiological responses.

SE focuses on observing and working with bodily sensations, helping the nervous system gradually regain its balance. We include all 5 dimensions: Sensations, Images, Behavior, Affect and Meaning.

Emotion-Focused Therapy (EFT)

Therapy takes place in an "ocean" of empathy. Designed to accelerate the process of accessing, expressing, and transforming emotions.

Emotions are not symptoms, but messengers that some emotional need is not being met.
